# DAC-IFEST-2024

Preliminary and Final Round submission for the **Data Analysis Competition (DAC)** at **Informatics Festival (IFest) 2024**, organized by Universitas Padjadjaran.

🏆 **Achievement:** 2nd Place  
👥 **Team:** Sudah Saatnya (Statistics UGM – Class of 2022)

---

## 📌 Overview

This project consists of two main phases:  
1. **Preliminary Round** – Focused on sanitation and clean water accessibility in Indonesia (SDG 6)  
2. **Final Round** – Focused on AI impact on jobs in Southeast Asia (SDG 8)  

Both projects aim to deliver **data-driven insights** to support policy-making and strategic decisions.

---

## 📊 Preliminary Round  
### *Improving Access to Safe Drinking Water in Indonesia*

This study analyzes and projects access to safe drinking water in Indonesia using machine learning and statistical modeling approaches.

### 🔍 Methods Used
- **Convolutional Neural Network (CNN)**  
  → Forecasting safe drinking water access (2000–2022)  
- **Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM)**  
  → Clustering regions based on water accessibility conditions  
- **Geographically Weighted Panel Regression (GWPR)**  
  → Identifying regional factors affecting sanitation and water access  

### 💡 Key Insights
- The projected access to safe drinking water is **unlikely to reach the 45% target by 2030**  
- Significant **regional disparities** exist in sanitation and water access  
- Localized factors play a critical role in determining accessibility  

📍 This study supports **SDG 6: Clean Water and Sanitation**

## 🤖 Final Round  
### *AI Impact Classification in Southeast Asia*

This project analyzes how artificial intelligence may impact jobs across Southeast Asia using machine learning techniques.

### 🔍 Approach
- Classification of jobs into:
  - Low Risk
  - Medium Risk
  - High Risk (automation impact)
- Identification of sectors most vulnerable to AI disruption  

### 💡 Key Insights
- Certain industries show **high exposure to automation risks**  
- Results can support **policy-making and workforce preparation strategies**  

📍 This study aligns with **SDG 8: Decent Work and Economic Growth**
